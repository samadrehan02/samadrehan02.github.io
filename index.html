Flagship Projects

AI Medical Scribe Research Open Source

Real-time transcription and structured clinical note generation from noisy Hindi/Hinglish doctor–patient conversations.

Research focus:
Studying hallucination control, structured generation, and error propagation in low-resource, code-switched medical ASR pipelines.

System overview:
Live audio stream → Hindi/Hinglish ASR → clinical entity extraction → schema-constrained LLM generation → structured OPD note export

  • Investigated entity-first pipelines as a mechanism to reduce hallucinated medical facts compared to end-to-end generation
  • Evaluated schema-constrained decoding vs free-form generation under noisy ASR and partial utterances
  • Designed session-scoped workflows with speaker handling for continuous, real-time clinical usage
  • Focused on failure visibility and auditability rather than maximizing offline benchmark scores

Outcomes:
• Significantly reduced invalid or hallucinated clinical entities under noisy ASR
• Improved consistency and structural validity of generated notes across sessions
• Identified normalization errors as a larger failure source than raw ASR WER

Constraints: real-time audio, mixed-language speech, clinical safety, low tolerance for hallucinations

Research focus:

Raw transcript → speaker normalization → structured information extraction → schema-constrained LLM summarization

  • Prioritized consistency and downstream usability over narrative fluency

Outcomes:

• Reduced variance in summaries compared to free-form generation

Constraints: noisy transcripts, overlapping speakers, implicit decisions, need for consistent structure

TCN Stock Prediction Research Open Source

Research focus:
Investigating hybrid architectures for balancing local temporal pattern capture with long-range dependency modeling.

Historical price data → feature normalization → TCN for short-term structure → Transformer for long-range dependencies

  • Designed strict data splits to reduce leakage and misleading evaluation

Outcomes:
• Achieved stable forecasting performance across repeated runs
