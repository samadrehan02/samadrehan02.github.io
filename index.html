AI Medical Scribe Research Open Source

Real-time transcription and structured clinical note generation from noisy Hindi/Hinglish doctor–patient conversations in outpatient settings.

Research focus:
Studying hallucination control, error propagation, and structured generation reliability in low-resource, code-switched medical ASR pipelines.

System overview:
Live audio → Hindi/Hinglish ASR → clinical entity extraction → schema-constrained LLM generation → structured OPD note export

  • Investigated entity-first pipelines as a mitigation strategy for hallucinated clinical facts compared to end-to-end generation
  • Evaluated schema-constrained decoding vs free-form generation under noisy ASR, partial utterances, and interruptions
  • Designed session-scoped workflows with speaker handling to support continuous, real-time clinical usage
  • Prioritized failure visibility and auditability over offline benchmark optimization

Outcomes:
• Significantly reduced invalid or hallucinated clinical entities
• Improved structural consistency of generated OPD notes across sessions
• Identified text normalization as a larger error source than raw ASR WER

Constraints: real-time audio, noisy environments, mixed-language speech, clinical safety, low tolerance for hallucinations
